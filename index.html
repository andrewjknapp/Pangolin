Pangolin

  • Appearance
  • Habitat

Appearance

Pangolins are the only mammal with true scales. There are many different species of pangolin which range in size from 8 inches in length to 30 inches. The giant pangolin can weigh as much as 73 pounds and the chinese pangolin can weigh as little as 8 pounds.

Habitat

Pangolins are found in south east Asia and south Africa. Their habitat is being threatened by human land development and they are poached for their meat and scales.

Ecology

Pangolins are carnivorous mammals which mainly consume insects such as ants and termites. They use their long tongue to reach into these insect's tunnels. Some pangolin's tongues are as long as 16 inches!
